# **6. Emergency Reasoning Framework**

Universal logic:
1. **Stabilize ABCs immediately.**  
2. Identify **red flags** (hypotension, AMS, focal deficits, bleeding, peritonitis, severe hypertension in pregnancy).  
3. Determine **time-sensitive diagnoses** (MI, stroke, ectopic rupture, eclampsia, sepsis).  
4. Order **appropriate labs/imaging** (synthetic only).  
5. Apply **risk scores** (Wells, CURB-style, conceptual ECG red flags).  
6. Classify severity and determine escalation needs.  
7. Initiate conceptual management tier (no doses, no prescribing).  
8. Reassess frequently.

**Pitfalls to emphasize:**
- Missing posterior stroke  
- Misinterpreting early sepsis  
- Confusing DKA vs HHS  
- Overusing D-dimer in high-risk PE  
- Assuming all chest pain = ACS  
- Missing RUQ pain → cholangitis  
- Delayed recognition of ectopic rupture  
- Overlooking severe features in preeclampsia
